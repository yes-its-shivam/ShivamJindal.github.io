Cosine Similarity & NLP

NLP or natural language processing is the branch of study in computer science which gives computers the ability to understand text and spoken words in much the same way human beings can. Cosine or in general term cos in vector theory refers to the measure of similarity between two nonzero vectors, and it is calculated by the dot product of the vectors divided by the product of their lengths.

When we talk about calculating similarity score between two words or sentences/phrases or even entire documents,it becames a tideous task but cosine similarity comes to the rescue, we can convert the language data into the form of vectors using Doc2Vec or SentenceBERT algorithms and then we can compute the cosine similarity scores between the vector or set of vectors. click to view my project on cosine similarity

Centroid Tracking in Computer Vision

So , what is a centroid? In mathematics we define a centroid as the absolute centre of a 2D object or absolute centre of gravity of a 3D object. In terms of computer vision everything we percieve through cameras is captured in 2D hence in terms of A.I. centroid is defined as the centre point of the object in the image or the video. Centroid tracking helps in making it possible for us to count unique objects in a video like building a person counter.

We define an object in the video by first detecting the object in the video and drawing a bounding box i.e. the mose immediate box that can be drawn in 2D plane that should contain the entire object, then we compute the centroid or in simple terms the center (x, y)-coordinates of the bounding box and then finishes the phase by alloting a unique ID to the current object. click to check my cnetroid tracking git repository

GPUs? what the heck are those?

Umm... all of us knows about CPUs yes the so called brain of our computer and is very much responsible to perform tasks in computer but GPUs? umm okay let me explain, in simple terms GPU is the part of our computer that supports parallel computing and is responsible for graphics and photo/video rendering.

Although GPUs are best known for their capabilities in gaming but, some of the most exciting applications for GPU technology involve AI and machine learning. Because GPUs incorporate an extraordinary amount of computational capability(parallel computing), they can deliver incredible acceleration in workloads that take advantage of the highly parallel nature of GPUs, such as image recognition, Deep learning, Computer Vision, and for that reason many cloud coding platforms like kaggle.com provides cloud GPU resources.
